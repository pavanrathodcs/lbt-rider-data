The Core Tension

Discount programs require giving up your privacy.

LBT charges the same base fare regardless of how you pay — but the financial benefits low-income riders depend on most are locked behind registered accounts.

💵
Option A
Anonymous Payment
Cash or unregistered TAP card
No name attached to your trips
Same base fare — no privacy premium
📋
Option B (required for discounts)
Registered Account
Required for LIFE, GoPass, and Reduced Fare programs
Collects name, contact info, DOB, and payment data
Travel history tied to verified identity
⚠️
The Ethical Problem: Riders who can afford full fare stay private. Riders who need discounts cannot. Privacy becomes something only the wealthy can afford.
The Stakes

Deleting your data means losing your discount.

California law allows TAP to retain rider data for years — and recent cyberattacks show that data is a real, growing target.

Recommendations

Four design changes that would
close the privacy gap.

GoPass already proves LACMTA can do better — extending its standards to all riders is a design choice, not a technical limitation.

01
Separate Eligibility from Transactions
Allow riders to delete travel history without losing LIFE or GoPass benefits. Eligibility documents and trip records should live in different systems.
→
02
Default to 2-Year Retention
Match the standard GoPass already applies to students; reduce breach exposure across the entire system, not just student accounts.
→
03
Plain-Language Privacy Summaries
Post readable summaries at validators and on buses, where consent actually happens. Privacy policies buried on websites don't count.
→
04
Promote Cash as the Privacy Choice
Cash is already free and anonymous — communicate that to riders who would benefit. The most privacy-preserving option shouldn't be the most invisible one.
→
In Closing

Public transit is the great equalizer.
Its data systems shouldn't undermine that.
